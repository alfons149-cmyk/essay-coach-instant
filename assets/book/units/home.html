Why practising with AI is not enough

1. The exam is offline

In the exam room, you cannot ask AI to “fix” your writing. You need to know how to structure your ideas, choose the right words, and avoid typical mistakes by yourself.

2. Real progress takes practice

You don’t learn to write Cambridge-level essays in a few days. It takes repeated practice, clear explanations, and targeted correction of your personal mistakes.

3. You must think like an examiner

Cambridge rewards specific skills: task achievement, organisation, range of language, and accuracy. This app and book show you exactly how they want you to write.
