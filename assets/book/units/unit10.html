Cohesion & Linking (Essay Level)

Useful for:

  • Cohesion between ideas
  • Overusing connectors

When you sit down to write your Cambridge essay, it is tempting to start writing immediately. You look at the question, think of a few ideas, and begin your first sentence.

But halfway through, you may realise your examples repeat, your ideas do not connect, or you have forgotten one of the notes. That is the danger of skipping the plan.

A few minutes of planning can transform your essay ‚Äì not by adding more words, but by giving every sentence a clear purpose.

1. Why Planning Matters

Many students think planning wastes time. In reality, it saves time ‚Äì and marks.

Without a plan, your essay often becomes:

  • unbalanced ‚Äì too much about one idea, not enough about another,
  • repetitive ‚Äì the same argument said twice,
  • unfinished ‚Äì you run out of time before the conclusion.

With a plan, you write faster, stay focused, and make fewer mistakes. Think of planning as building the map before starting your journey. You would not drive to an unknown city without checking where you are going first.

2. How Much Time to Spend

In the B2 exam, you have about 40 minutes for the essay. A good time plan is:

  • 5‚Äì7 minutes: plan your ideas and structure,
  • 25 minutes: write your essay,
  • 5 minutes: check and correct.

Those first few minutes of planning are crucial. They make your writing smoother, more precise, and more confident.

3. Step 1 ‚Äì Read the Task Carefully

Look at this real-style question:

Example task

In your English class you have been talking about how to stay healthy. Now your teacher has asked you to write an essay.

Write an essay using all the notes and giving reasons for your point of view.

Some people say that the best way to stay healthy is to do regular exercise.

Notes:

  • 1. diet
  • 2. stress
  • 3. your own idea

This looks simple ‚Äì but many students lose marks because they do not read carefully. You must use all the notes (1, 2, and your own idea). That means your essay needs at least three arguments: one about diet, one about stress, and one extra idea.

Tip: Before you start writing, underline or circle key words in the question. It helps you focus on what you must include.

4. Step 2 ‚Äì Analyse the Question

Before you think of ideas, ask yourself:

  1. What is the topic? ‚Üí How to stay healthy.
  2. What is the main question? ‚Üí Is regular exercise the best way?
  3. What are the three notes? ‚Üí diet, stress, your own idea.

Once you understand this, you already have your essay outline:

  • Paragraph 1: Introduction (state the topic and opinion)
  • Paragraph 2: First note (diet)
  • Paragraph 3: Second note (stress) + your own idea
  • Paragraph 4: Conclusion

You have not written a word yet, but your structure is already taking shape.

5. Step 3 ‚Äì Brainstorm Ideas

Now comes the creative part. Before you write, spend one minute quickly noting down any ideas that come to mind for each note.

  • Diet: eating too much fast food ‚Üí unhealthy; balanced diet helps energy; avoid sugar; governments should educate people.
  • Stress: causes illness, poor sleep, anxiety; relaxation helps; doing sports reduces stress.
  • Your own idea: sleep and rest are vital; mental health; social connections; spending time outside.

Write ideas fast ‚Äì do not judge them yet. The goal is to get ideas out of your head and onto paper. You can choose the best ones later.

6. Step 4 ‚Äì Choose Your Main Points

Once you have a list, select one strong point for each note ‚Äì something you can explain clearly in two or three sentences.

Example choices

  • Diet: A healthy diet gives the body energy and prevents illness.
  • Stress: Exercise helps reduce stress and makes people feel better.
  • Own idea: Enough rest and sleep are just as important as physical activity.

Now you have your three main points. They will become the heart of your essay.

7. Step 5 ‚Äì Plan the Paragraphs (The Mini-Outline)

Turn your points into a quick, visual plan. Your plan should be short ‚Äì no full sentences, just notes.

Sample mini-outline

Essay question: Best way to stay healthy ‚Üí exercise?

  • Introduction: introduce topic and opinion ‚Äì many ways to stay healthy; exercise important but not the only factor.
  • Paragraph 1 (diet): balanced diet ‚Üí energy, immunity; fast food causes problems.
  • Paragraph 2 (stress): exercise reduces stress; mental health benefits.
  • Paragraph 3 (own idea): enough sleep and rest; emotional wellbeing; balance.
  • Conclusion: exercise is important, but real health = body + mind balance.

That is your map. It takes about five minutes, but now you can write smoothly for 25 minutes without stopping to think, ‚ÄúWhat is next?‚Äù

8. Step 6 ‚Äì Add Connectors and Transitions

Once your ideas are chosen, decide how to connect them. Smooth transitions make your essay coherent and easy to follow.

Use linking words you already know:

  • Adding ideas: moreover, in addition, also, what is more
  • Contrasting: however, on the other hand, although, whereas
  • Explaining results: therefore, as a result, consequently
  • Giving examples: for example, for instance
  • Summarising: in conclusion, to sum up, overall

You do not need to write these words into your plan, but imagining where they will go will make your writing more cohesive.

8.1 Avoid overusing connectors

Connectors are helpful, but too many can make your writing feel heavy and mechanical. You do not need a linking word in every sentence.

Example (too many connectors):

Firstly, exercise is important. Secondly, it helps people relax. Thirdly, it also improves sleep. Finally, it is good for social life.

Improved:

Exercise is important because it keeps the body strong and healthy. It also helps people relax and sleep better, and it can improve their social life.

Use connectors when they add clarity, not just to fill space.

8.2 Use a variety of linking words

Repeating the same connector again and again (for example, always using also) makes your essay sound basic. Show range by using different, natural alternatives.

Instead of:

Also‚Ä¶ Also‚Ä¶ Also‚Ä¶

Try:

Furthermore‚Ä¶ In addition‚Ä¶ What is more‚Ä¶

A small variety in linking words immediately makes your writing feel more advanced and more cohesive.

9. Step 7 ‚Äì Use ‚ÄúIdea Builders‚Äù

When brainstorming, it helps to think in simple idea-building formulas. They help you expand your points logically without repeating yourself.

(a) Cause ‚Üí Effect ‚Üí Example

Eating a balanced diet keeps the body strong ‚Üí it prevents disease ‚Üí for example, people who eat more vegetables usually have fewer health problems.

(b) Problem ‚Üí Solution

Stress affects health negatively ‚Üí regular exercise helps release tension ‚Üí this improves concentration and mood.

(c) General ‚Üí Specific

Good health depends on balance ‚Üí this means caring for both body and mind ‚Üí rest and social connection are just as important as exercise.

10. Step 8 ‚Äì Plan Your Opinion

In a Cambridge essay, you must give your opinion, but you can decide where to show it:

  • in the introduction (e.g. Personally, I believe‚Ä¶)
  • in the conclusion (e.g. For this reason, I think‚Ä¶)
  • or both ‚Äì start neutral and finish with a clear view.

Example

Introduction:

Many people think exercise is the best way to stay healthy, but there are other important factors.

Conclusion:

In my view, exercise is essential, but it should be combined with good nutrition and enough rest.

Balanced opinions sound more thoughtful ‚Äì and Cambridge examiners appreciate that. Avoid extreme, emotional statements like ‚ÄúPeople who don‚Äôt exercise are lazy.‚Äù

11. Step 9 ‚Äì Visualise Your Essay

Now that you have your plan, imagine how it will look on the page. Each paragraph has its own job ‚Äì no more, no less.

Structure overview

  • Introduction (~30 words): general topic; mention both sides; state your opinion.
  • Body paragraph 1 (~50 words): diet ‚Äì balanced meals, energy, prevention of illness; example.
  • Body paragraph 2 (~50 words): stress ‚Äì exercise reduces tension and anxiety; example.
  • Body paragraph 3 (~50 words): own idea ‚Äì rest, mental health, emotional balance; example.
  • Conclusion (~25 words): true health = combination of factors; final thought or recommendation.

12. Step 10 ‚Äì Avoid Overplanning

Some students fall into the opposite trap: they plan too much. They write full sentences, arrows, and long lists ‚Äì and then have no time to write the essay itself.

Your plan should be short and visual ‚Äì a sketch, not a sculpture. If you can glance at it and immediately know what to write next, it is perfect.

You do not get marks for your plan, but it is the foundation for everything that follows.

13. Step 11 ‚Äì Stay Flexible

Even with a plan, your ideas may change while writing ‚Äì and that is fine. The plan is a guide, not a prison.

If a new idea appears that fits the question better, include it ‚Äì but stay logical. Never skip one of the notes, and always check your word count.

Examiners like essays that feel natural, not memorised.

14. Step 12 ‚Äì Practise Planning Only

Before your next practice essay, try this exercise: plan without writing.

Take five different essay questions and spend exactly five minutes on each plan. For example:

  • School should focus more on creativity than exams.
  • The best way to learn a language is by living in the country where it is spoken.
  • Technology has improved communication, but it has also made people more isolated.
  • Young people should do unpaid work to help their community.
  • Happiness depends on money.

After five plans, you will notice patterns: how to organise, balance arguments, and find logical examples quickly. That is what turns you into a confident exam writer.

15. From Plan to Writing

Once your plan is ready, writing becomes almost automatic. You already know what each paragraph will say ‚Äì all that is left is to join the dots with your sentence and paragraph skills.

When you plan, you do not just prepare your essay; you prepare your thinking. You decide what is important, what comes first, and what supports your message.

Remember: writing without a plan is like running a race without a map. Planning gives you direction, balance, and confidence.

16. Quick Self-Check Before Writing

Before you begin your essay, ask yourself:

  • ‚úÖ Did I understand the question?
  • ‚úÖ Did I include all three notes?
  • ‚úÖ Do I know what each paragraph will say?
  • ‚úÖ Do I have one clear opinion?
  • ‚úÖ Do I have time to finish?

If all five answers are yes ‚Äì start writing. You are ready.

17. Final Reflection

Planning is not a waste of time ‚Äì it is the secret to clarity. It is what separates a rushed essay from a focused, confident one.

You do not need to write more to score higher; you need to plan better. Every minute you spend organising your ideas adds calm, logic, and flow to your writing.

That calmness is what the examiner feels when reading your essay ‚Äì and it is what earns you the higher band.

Remember: plan first, write second, succeed third.

Summary & Quick Reference

Unit 10 shows you how to plan a B2 essay so that your writing is clear, organised, and easier to write under time pressure. A good plan does not need full sentences; it just needs clear decisions.

1) The 3‚ÄìStep Planning Process

  1. Understand the task
    ‚Üí What is the topic?
    ‚Üí What question do you need to answer?
    ‚Üí Do you need to choose an opinion, discuss advantages/disadvantages, or give a solution?
  2. Choose your position
    ‚Üí Do you mostly agree, mostly disagree, or have a balanced view?
    ‚Üí Decide this before you start.
  3. Make a quick outline
    ‚Üí Intro = topic + question + your opinion
    ‚Üí Body 1 = main reason 1 (+ example)
    ‚Üí Body 2 = main reason 2 (+ example / contrast)
    ‚Üí Conclusion = short summary + restate opinion

Plan = 4 lines: Intro ‚Äì Reason 1 ‚Äì Reason 2 ‚Äì Conclusion

2) A Sample B2 Essay Plan

Task: ‚ÄúSome people think homework is necessary; others say it is a waste of time. Write an essay giving your opinion.‚Äù

  • Intro: Homework is a common part of school; people disagree about its value. (My view: homework is useful if not excessive.)
  • Body 1: Helps students practise what they learned in class ‚Äì improves memory and exam results. Example: revising grammar or vocabulary at home.
  • Body 2: Can be stressful if there is too much; families have less free time. However, reasonable homework teaches responsibility.
  • Conclusion: Homework is helpful when teachers give a fair amount and clear tasks.

Notice: short notes, no full sentences, but the whole essay is already decided.

3) Timing Guide (for a 40-minute writing task)

  • Planning: 5‚Äì7 minutes
  • Writing: about 25 minutes
  • Checking: 5‚Äì8 minutes (spelling, punctuation, linking words)

If you skip the planning stage, you often lose more time later trying to fix the structure.

4) Useful ‚ÄúPlanning Phrases‚Äù for B2 Essays

Introductions:

  • ‚ÄúMany people believe that‚Ä¶‚Äù
  • ‚ÄúNowadays, there is a lot of discussion about‚Ä¶‚Äù
  • ‚ÄúIn this essay, I will discuss‚Ä¶‚Äù (safe at B2)

Giving reasons:

  • ‚ÄúOne important reason is that‚Ä¶‚Äù
  • ‚ÄúAnother advantage is that‚Ä¶‚Äù
  • ‚ÄúOn the other hand, some people argue that‚Ä¶‚Äù

Conclusions:

  • ‚ÄúIn conclusion, I believe that‚Ä¶‚Äù
  • ‚ÄúTo sum up, it seems that‚Ä¶‚Äù
  • ‚ÄúOverall, the benefits outweigh the drawbacks because‚Ä¶‚Äù

5) Common Planning Mistakes

  • No plan at all: ideas become repeated or unbalanced.
  • Too many ideas: five or six small points, none of them properly developed.
  • Changing opinion halfway: the introduction and conclusion do not match.
  • Writing the whole essay in the plan: you waste time; use notes and arrows only.

Safe rule: choose 2 strong reasons and develop them clearly.

6) Mini Practice ‚Äì Plan in 3 Lines

Topic: ‚ÄúMore and more people are working from home. Do you think this is a positive or negative development?‚Äù

Try to write a 3‚Äìline plan in your notebook:

  1. Intro ‚Üí your opinion in one short phrase
  2. Body 1 ‚Üí main advantage (+ quick example)
  3. Body 2 ‚Üí main disadvantage / limit (+ quick example)

If your plan is clear, writing the essay will feel more relaxed and controlled.
