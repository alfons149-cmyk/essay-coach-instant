Unit 6

Cohesion and Coherence

In the previous unit, you learned how to build strong individual paragraphs. In this unit, you will learn how to make your paragraphs work together so that the whole text feels connected and easy to read.

Examiners call this cohesion (how sentences are linked) and coherence (how clearly the overall message is organised).

1. What Is Cohesion?

Cohesion is the way you connect sentences and paragraphs so that the text feels like one continuous piece, not separate parts.

You create cohesion by using:

  • reference words (it, they, this, such a problem…)
  • substitution (do so, one, ones)
  • linking phrases (however, in addition, as a result…)
  • consistent vocabulary for the same ideas.

Low cohesion:

Online learning is popular. Online learning is convenient. Online learning is also flexible.

Improved cohesion:

Online learning is popular because it is convenient. It is also flexible, which makes it easier for people with busy schedules to study.

2. Reference and Substitution

Instead of repeating the same words again and again, good writers use reference words and substitution to keep the text smooth.

2.1 Reference

Common reference words:

  • pronouns: it, they, this, that, these, those
  • determiners: such, the former, the latter
  • noun phrases: this issue, this trend, this problem

Without reference:

Social media can be useful for staying in contact with friends. Social media can also be a distraction, and social media may reduce face-to-face communication.

With reference:

Social media can be useful for staying in contact with friends. However, it can also be a distraction, and this may reduce face-to-face communication.

2.2 Substitution

Substitution means replacing a word or phrase with a shorter one to avoid repetition.

  • do so – repeat an action: Some students read every day, but others don’t do so.
  • one / ones – repeat a noun: Online courses can be helpful, especially short ones.

3. Linking Within and Between Paragraphs

You already know connectors such as however and therefore. Here, we focus on how to use them to link ideas smoothly.

Inside a paragraph

Use linkers to show:

  • addition: in addition, moreover, furthermore
  • contrast: however, on the other hand, nevertheless
  • result: therefore, as a result, consequently
  • example: for example, for instance

Between paragraphs

At the beginning of a paragraph, you can use a short phrase to connect it to what came before.

End of Paragraph 1:

One advantage of online learning is flexibility, as students can decide when to study.

Beginning of Paragraph 2:

However, this flexibility can also be a disadvantage, because it is easy for students to lose discipline.

4. Creating a Smooth Flow

Coherence is not only about linking words; it is about the order of your ideas.

Typical paragraph progression:

  1. Topic sentence – main point.
  2. Explanation – why / how.
  3. Example – support.
  4. Mini-conclusion / link to the next paragraph.

Mini-conclusion and link:

In this way, regular reading not only improves language level but also develops deeper concentration. In the next section, we will see how this affects exam performance.

Small phrases like in this way, for this reason, or as a result help your writing feel logically connected.

5. Practice

Task 1 – Add reference words

Improve cohesion by replacing repeated words with pronouns or “this + noun”.

Original:

Online learning is becoming more popular. Online learning allows students to study from home. Online learning also reduces travel costs.

Your version: Use it, this, or this option to avoid repetition.

Task 2 – Link two paragraphs

Write two short body paragraphs about the advantages and disadvantages of social media.

  • Use at least one linking phrase at the start of the second paragraph.
  • Use reference words instead of repeating “social media” every time.

6. Final Thoughts

Cohesion and coherence are what make your essay feel like one complete piece of writing. By using reference words, substitution, and clear linking between paragraphs, you help the examiner follow your argument without effort.

In the next units, we will focus on specific Cambridge tasks and see how to apply these skills to introductions, conclusions, and full essay structures.

14. Summary & Quick Reference

Unit 6 showed how careful word choice can lift your writing from “correct” to convincing and advanced. Use this page as a quick check when you edit your essays.

Core ideas

  • Cambridge rewards range (variety) and precision (exact meaning).
  • Avoid weak intensifiers like very when a stronger word exists.
  • Use collocations and strong verbs to sound natural and formal.
  • Choose the right register – no slang in formal essays.
  • Be concise: remove unnecessary words and vague expressions.

Upgrade weak adjectives

Replace very + adjective with one stronger word:

  • very big → huge, enormous, massive
  • very small → tiny, minute
  • very tired → exhausted, drained
  • very happy → delighted, thrilled
  • very angry → furious, livid
  • very good → excellent, outstanding
  • very bad → awful, terrible, dreadful

Weak: The results were very good.

Better: The results were excellent.

Gradable vs. non-gradable

Gradable adjectives (tired, cold, happy):

a bit, quite, very, extremely + adjective

She is very tired.

Non-gradable adjectives (exhausted, freezing, perfect):

absolutely, completely, totally, utterly + adjective

She was absolutely exhausted after the exam.

❌ very exhausted

✅ absolutely exhausted

Strong verbs & formal choices

1. Replace weak verb phrases:

  • make better → improve
  • make worse → worsen / damage
  • have an influence on → influence / affect
  • do research → conduct research
  • put into practice → apply

❌ The government must make better rules.

✅ The government must improve its regulations.

2. Formal alternatives to phrasal verbs:

  • find out → discover
  • give up → abandon, quit
  • come up with → propose, suggest
  • put off → postpone, delay
  • look into → investigate, examine
  • deal with → address, tackle

❌ We need to look into this problem.

✅ We need to investigate this problem.

Register & conciseness

For essays, prefer neutral/formal vocabulary:

  • kids → children
  • a lot of → many, numerous
  • stuff / things → items, issues, aspects
  • talk about → discuss
  • because of → due to, owing to

❌ A lot of kids get bad grades because of problems at home.

✅ Many children receive low grades due to problems at home.

Quick self-check

  • Did I avoid overusing very?
  • Have I used strong adjectives and strong verbs where possible?
  • Is my register appropriate (no slang, no chatty phrases)?
  • Are my sentences concise, without unnecessary padding?
  • Are my synonyms correct for the context, not just “advanced”?

Try this: Take one paragraph from an old essay and rewrite it by: 1) removing very, 2) replacing weak verbs, and 3) making the register more formal.

7. Summary & Quick Reference

Core ideas

  • Cohesion = links between sentences and paragraphs.
  • Coherence = clear overall organisation of ideas.
  • Use reference words (it, this, they) instead of repeating nouns.
  • Use linkers to show addition, contrast, result, and example.

Typical exam problems

  • Repeating the same noun in every sentence.
  • Using linkers randomly or too often.
  • Paragraphs that do not clearly connect to each other.
  • Sudden topic changes without explanation.

Useful patterns

  • Reference: This trend / This issue / This change
  • Contrast: However, On the other hand, In contrast
  • Result: Therefore, As a result, Consequently
  • Link forward: For this reason, In the next section…

Quick self-check

  • Do I avoid repeating the same words unnecessarily?
  • Can the reader clearly see how each paragraph follows from the previous one?
  • Have I used linkers naturally, not in every sentence?
  • Does the text feel like one continuous message?
